# Inclusive Research Methods

Adapted from inclusive-design-skills (MC Dean).

## Recruitment
- Include participants with disabilities (motor, visual, auditory, cognitive)
- Include age diversity (don't default to 25-34 tech workers)
- Include assistive technology users
- Compensate fairly — accessibility needs may require higher compensation
- Offer multiple participation formats (in-person, remote, async)

## Session accommodations
- Ask about needs in advance (captioning, breaks, materials format)
- Allow extra time (1.5x standard for some participants)
- Provide materials in accessible formats
- Have a backup plan if technology fails
- Never ask participants to demonstrate their disability

## Analysis considerations
- Don't collapse accessibility findings into "edge cases"
- Distinguish between a11y barriers and usability issues
- Weight severity by impact (a complete blocker for one person
  outweighs mild annoyance for many)
- Report findings per disability category, not aggregated

## Principle: "Nothing about us without us"
Include people with disabilities in research about accessibility.
Don't design accessibility features based only on simulation or
non-disabled perspectives.
